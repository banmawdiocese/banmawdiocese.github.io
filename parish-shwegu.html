Brief Description of Shwegu Parish

Shwegu Parish was formerly part of Banmaw Parish. After the creation of Banmaw Diocese, it was reaffirmed as a quasi-parish by Bishop Raymond Sumlut Gam in 2007 and Rev. Fr. Francis Saw Phay Han was appointed as the parish priest. Later it was promoted to full parish and at present, the parish is under the care of Rev. Fr. Paschal Hpaudau Tu Ja. Together with the parish priest, four religious sisters (SFX), one religious brother (SFX) and (16) catechists are working for the local church.

Principal Church

St. Peter's Church is the principal Church of the Parish which was finished and blessed in the year 2006.

Statistic:

About 320 Catholic families with 1943 parishioners are living and sharing catholic faith in Shwegu Parish. The parish consists (14) villages.
