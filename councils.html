Diocesan Councils

Diocese of Banmaw

 

Presbyteral Council (Priests' Senate):

Presbyteral Council assists and advises the Bishop in the governance of the diocese, on behalf of the local presbyterate, on the pastoral welfare of the people of God and other matters described in the Code of Canon Law. In Banmaw Diocese, a new Council was constituted again in 2018 with eleven Council members including the Bishop. Constitution, membership and conducting council's meetings are governed by the approved Statutes of the Council.

Pastoral Council:

Diocesan Pastoral Council is an advisory Council to the Diocesan Bishop. It carefully investigates, prayerfully considers and, in consensus, recommends action to the Bishop regarding pastoral concerns facing the Diocese. (Decree on the Pastoral Office of Bishops 27) 
This assembly operates in accordance with Canon Law (cc. 511-514) and the approved Statutes of the Council. This confidential forum is invaluable to the Bishop in discerning how to best promote the Gospel in light of the pastoral needs of the day. The Council consists members by their offices, representatives of different institutions and appointed memebers for particular feilds, and it is for a term of four years.

College of Consultors (Priests' Council):

Diocesan Bishop elects personally a number of priests from the Presbyteral Council and consitutes the College of Consultors. He presides over it and seeks advice from the Consultors in the more practical and ordinary administration of the diocese according to the norms set by Canon Law or when he deems necessary. In Banmaw Diocese, the College of Consultors was constituted again in 2018 with eight Council members including the Bishop.
