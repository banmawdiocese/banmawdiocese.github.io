 Mansi Parish

Parish General Info

Brief Description

Mansi Parish was formerly part of Banmaw Parish. It was then established as a new parish by Bishop Raymond Sumlut Gam on June 14, 2007. The first Parish Priest was Fr. John Yung Wa, and after him, Fr. Peter Lazum Tu Lum. At present, the parish is under the care of Rev. Fr. Luke Lahpai Hka Li. Together with him, four religious sisters (FMM) and one religious brother (SFX) are serving the local church.

Principal Church

St. Joseph's Church is the principal Church of the Parish which was one of the historical Church in the Diocese. Due to the need, a new Church is beeing built in its place.

Statistic:

About 406 Catholic families with 2261 parishioners are living and sharing christian faith in Mansi Parish. It has 10 villages with 9 Catechists.

Our Pastor

Rev. Fr. Luke Lahpai Hka Li was appointed as the Parish Priest of Mansi in May 2019. Formerly he was assigned as the Director of Social Communication Office and then, as the Parish Priest of Zau Bung parish.
