Parishes in Vicariates

A parish is defined as â€œa community of Christ's faithful, stably established within a particular church, whose pastoral care, under the authority of the diocesan bishop, is entrusted to a parish priest as its proper pastor" (Canon 511). A parish is generally territorial and can include one or more churches. The parish embraces all Christ's faithful of a given geographical area (Canon 518). 

Only the diocesan bishop, after consultation with his Council of Priests, can establish, suppress or alter parishes (Canon 515.2).

We have thirteen Parishes in Banmaw Diocese which are zoned into two Vicariates under respective Vicars Forane,
Rev. Fr. Michael Lazum Lung Wa and Rev. Fr. Luke Lahpai Hka Li. However, due to decreasing numbers of faithful and limited resources, the care of Sinlum Parish has been placed under the administration of Momauk Parish Priest.
