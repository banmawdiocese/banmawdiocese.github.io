Brief Description

Laiza parish locates far north-east part of Banmaw and at the adge of the boundary of the Diocese. It is geographically and politically important place, which became the center administrative town of Kachin Independence Organization (KIO) in 1994, and is right on the borderline of Myanmaw and China.

Before the creation of Banmaw Diocese, it was part of of Nam Sam Yang Parish which is now in the Diocese of Myitkyina. After the creation of the Diocese, seeing the significance of the place and pastoral needs of the people, Laiza was created as quasi-parish first in 2007 and later, as a full parish in 2009. The first parish priest of Laiza was Rev. Fr. Josehp Nbwi Naw who is regarded as the founder of the parish. He was always with the people, especially in difficult time of civil war. Till now he stay at the parish but as a retired priest and helping the people.

At present, Laiza parish is under the care of Rev. Fr. Peter Lazum Tu Lum who was assigned as the parish priest on April 23, 2019. Three religious Sisters of St. Francis Xavier are also helping the priest and working for the parish.

Principal Church

Christ the King Church is the principal Church at Laiza Parish which was built by Rev. Fr. Joseph Nbwi Naw and blessed in the year of 2005.
