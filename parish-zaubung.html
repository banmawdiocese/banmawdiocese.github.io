Brief Description of Zau Bung Parish

Zau Bung parish first located in Zau Bung village and consisted (39) remote mountain villages. Originally, the center of the Parish was at Zau Bung village. And Columban Missionary Priest, Rev. Fr. Divene was the first to stay at the village and start mission there. But due to jealousy and animosity by other local denomination, parish properties were destroyed and priests were frightened, and later, the parish center was moved to Man Wing Gyi village, which is on the highway road from Banmaw to Nam Hkan and 58 miles away from Banmaw.

After Columban Missionaries left the mission and Country, Fr. Rosena (1974) was the first indigenous priest to take care of the parish. Then, Fr. Gebriel Ba Tan (shortly in 1980), Fr. Joseph Gawlu Gam (1982-92), Fr. Joseph Awng Li (1992-2000), Fr. Stephen Zaw Min Latt (2003-2009), Fr. Luke Hka Li (2009-2014), Fr. John Jangma Yung Wa (2014-2019) administered the parish as the Parish Priests.

At present, Zau Bung parish is under the administration of Rev. Fr. Raymond Lazum La Di who was appointed as the parish priest on March 25, 2019. Four religious Sisters of St. Teresa's Little Way are also helping the priest and working for the parish.

Principal Church

St. Joseph's Church is the principal Church at Zau Bung Parish which was rebuilt by Rev. Fr. John Jangma Yung Wa and blessed in the year of 2019.
