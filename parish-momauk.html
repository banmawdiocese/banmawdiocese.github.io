Parish General Info

Brief Description

Momauk Parish is located (10) miles away from Banmaw (Bhamo) town, and it is one of four administrative townships in Kachin State. Created upon the arrival of Columban Missionary, Fr. Patrick Usher in name, who started the mission in 1943 with only six families, Momauk Parish is one of the ordest local church in the Diocese. Fr. Dennis McClindon was the first parish priest of Momauk who administered the local community in 1945, and till now, (16) priests had served the community.

Franciscan Missionary of Mary (FMM) were invited by Fr. Gerald O'Brein to help the mission and pastoral needs of the people, and Sr. Nathalee Gawlu Kaw Lum, Sr. Delhode Nangzing Kai and Sr. Mary Zinghtung Lu arrived on July 5, 1970. By this time, there have already been (43) FMM Sisters from their first arrival.

The early missionary work of Columban Priests and FMM Sisters had been blessed, and the first six christian families multiplied and increased to (724) families with total number of (3,829) catholic faithful. At present, Rev. Fr. Stephen Zaw Min Latt, the Parish Priest, with the help of Rev. Fr. John Zau Yaw and together with eight FMM Sisters and two SFX Brothers, is administering the parish and giving pastoral care to the people.

Principal Church

St. Patrick's Church is the principal Church for Momauk Parish which was built at the place of the Old Church by former parish priest, Rev. Fr. Joseph Laban Tu, and blessed in the year of 2009.

Statistic:

About 724 catholic families with 3,829 parishioners are living and sharing catholic faith in Momauk Parish. It has (9) communities at different parts of the parish and (23) Catechists are living with them.

Our Pastors
